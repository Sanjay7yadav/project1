New Design of Lord Ram Temple

The new plan for Ayodhya Ram Temple reveals the temple design as consisting of the three-storey stone structure that is built on an elevated platform with multiple pillars, turrets, and domes. In the new layout developed by the trust, the Ram temple has got still more grandeur, as compared to the original design that was established in 1988 by the Sompura family of Ahmedabad. At present, the Ayodhya’s Ram Mandir will be 235 feet by 360 feet with a height of 161 feet and has sketched following the ‘Nagara‘ style of architecture. As compared to the original temple plan, which consisted of only two domes, the new layout consists of five domes and a tower or shikhara that will build over the sanctum sanctorum. ......

The basic structure of a Hindu temple contains the following architectural details:

1. Garbhagriha – the small room where the principal deity/deities of the temple remain

2. Mandapa – the patio or hall at the entrance of the temple usually designed to house a massive number of people

3. Shikhara – the mountain like a tower which can have different forms from pyramidal to curvilinear

4. Vahana – the mount of the main deity placed usually in the line of sight from Garbhagriha

Puratan Ram Ji ki Paidi

Ram ki Paidi is a series of ghats on the bank of River Saryu in Ayodhya in the Indian state of Uttar Pradesh. A large number of pilgrims visit every day to take a sacred bath in holy Saryu waters. The river front brings forth a majestic landscape specially in floodlit night. There were enormous bathing ghats throughout the banks of the holy river Sarayu and the ones at Nayaghat were immersed in the heavy rains and floods. These were rebuilt in 1985.
